EGGS LOCATIONS

THE HEXAGON

Headquarters. If you don't know what that is, use a dictionary.


    Like the Pentagon, but better. This is where the main offices of EGGS are. In an undisclosed location, the most senior members of EGGS hierarchy file paperwork, coordinate missions, relay communications, think up mind-bending plans, etc. Director Robert Michaels, Vice Director Lily Warden and the five heads of departments all rub elbows here. The walls lean outwards, making it just that tiny bit harder for TASTY agents to scale, if they ever find the building in the first place. 



Archdirector Robert Michaels' Office

    

    This is one of the most highly secured places in the Hexagon. It has all the security measures you would expect from a spy organization, and then some. With it's back to the door, a cozy white desk, and a fake-leather swivelly chair stands behind it. It's high black neck rest seems to preside over the rest of the room, and if you lean in and take a deep breath you can catch a whiff of hair gel. No matter it's unassuming appearance, if need be it can travel up to 80 kph, powered by a small engine the Science division devised. Fantastic way to win office chair races! Behind it, a one-way glass forms the western wall, offering a breath-taking view of the sunset. 



The Halo-The helipad. The letter H wore off, leaving a bright yellow circle.


    Situated on the Hexagon roof, this is where agents meet to leave for far-away assignments. At night, it's illuminated by landing-strip lights. Every year at the EGGS Sports Day, teams fly a kilometer up into the air and have three hours to design a parachute that allows an egg to touch down unscathed with only newspaper, tape, string, pipe cleaners, a plastic bag and straws. The Science team has a twenty-three year winning streak. Losing teams have to clean the eggs up, and raw eggs are used. 



Lamp Lady's Lair-A tribute to Florence Nightingale. 


    Where injured agents come to heal. The "lair" is actually the entire Block C, boasting five floors, one for the operating theatres, two for wards, one for offices and another for physical therapy. Each ward is named for a different famous nurse, making it a bit confusing for people who don't know their history to navigate-they're in chronological order. Behind the reception desk is a framed portrait of Florence Nightingale, watching over all who enter. Agent Luke Cone holds the record for the most visits to date, with 394 trips in the bag. 



The Lion's Den-Vice Director Lily Warden's office. She has a tendency to bite the head off idiots.


    For convenience's sake, the door is twice the width of a regular one. The floor is astroturf, lined with ruts from the weight of a wheelchair. A mahogany desk ornamented with coffee mug stains, minus a chair poises on a heavy woven carpet, with an alarm button concealed on the underside. Her window wall, unlike the Director's, faces east. As opposite as people can be, they are each other's yin and yang. Many junior agents have placed bets on when they will get together. She likes to wake early and sleep early, while he is not a morning person and does paperwork until late at night, like their office windows. 



The Wall of Honour


    Walking through the glass entry doors of the Hexagon, this is the first thing you see. A gleaming wall with engraved bronze plaques, bearing the names who have fallen in combat. The very first one is ANTHEA NICOLE COULTAIN-ROGERS 1955. Among them are JÄGER DOVE 1980, MARIAN MAYHAND 1991, and DART FOSTER 2013. Every year on November 11, agents everywhere stop and take a minute to remember those who sacrificed their lives for EGGS. 



The Wishing Well-Ahh, so serene.


    First of all, it's not actually a well. It's actually a fountain, but the name stuck after training one day when a greenie was so thirsty he drank from the decorative fountain instead of the designated water fountain twenty metres away. The wishing part comes from when on August 19, the day when fourth-year trainees begin their first day of work, started to throw coins in wishing to do well and not muck up. At the end of each month, the coins are collected and put into the EGGS funds. Once, a whopping $6,873 was counted. The Wishing Well is a favourite lunch spot.


TASTY LOCATIONS

This is what we, EGGS, have managed to piece together from intel gathered from spies and TASTY agents.

Alcatraz-Their interrogation/prison facilities.
    We don't have a lot of intel on this. We discovered it by accident when Hatch was taken prisoner and we tracked her there, and she doesn't want to speak about it anytime soon.

Brainwashers-Where they train the new recruits. They call it the Institute.
    They have barracks at KG5, German Swiss International, an unknown Woodlands preschool and at Glenealy. There may be more, but we don't have confirmation. Glenealy is the biggest barracks, as it's built on a humongous mountain-lots of earth to tunnel under! 

The Factory Farms-Where did you think they got their meat?
    Think of a factory farm. Now multiply the stink, noise, filth, crowd and all-round terror and you have your basic TASTY farm. They are strategically placed under schools in the countryside, with less pipelines and wires running underground. Tiny fist-sized holes allow the bare minimum of vitamin D to be produced. The life expectancy for animals in one of these factories is 40% lower than the average.

Fund-raising Stations-Where they get their funds.
    Every. Single. Fast. Food. Shop. In. The. World.

Headquarters-Every organization has one.
    Every TASTY agent has rather died than give this away, but we have one clue: the last words forced out of an agent were "It's at S-" and then anti-climatically, he died.

HISTORIES

    It all started on April 15th, 1955 when Nikola MacCartson, a young reporter, went to review a new restaurant called McDonald. Digging deep beneath the surface, she uncovered horrifying tales of animals being experimented on and genetically modified in a quest to create the tastiest meat, mass bred, treated like unfeeling objects and other terrible facts. She discovered they were affiliated with KFC, then already a flourishing food chain, and Burger King, a new up-and-rising commerce. Their alliance was christened "TASTY". MacCartson prepared a powerful article, but the newspaper she worked for rejected it, saying it was "too disturbing for public eyes". Unable to just stand by and watch, she decided to do something, creating the birth of EGGS.

    Knowing she could never combat such popular companies openly, she searched for like-minded people, first meeting up in in-the-wall restaurants, then in each other's houses and by 1960 when there were enough members, office rooms. They lived double lives, normal working class citizens by day, fast-food rebels by night. They would sneak into enemy offices and document incriminating paperwork, releasing it to the public in attempts to warn them. They protested, boycotted, put posters up,and handed out leaflets. The public listened for a while, however there were always people who went back to eat more. 

    By 1963, MacCartson realized that to defeat TASTY, they needed trained recruits. So she started up the Academy. Divided into five divisions (Administration, Communications, Field, Science and Life-savers), trainees studied the area they had the most aptitude for, at first undergoing two years of training and when new information was released and EGGS received more funding, the training period was extended to four years. Oregano Caning became Head of Administration, Jack Tripousa became Head of Communications, Jäger Dove became the Head of Field, Fritz Simon became the Head of Science and Douglas Doolie became Head of Life-savers. 

    The Battle of Beruloo took place in 1965. EGGS had just discovered a massive experimentation site and agents were flocking into the final showdown. Tension was in the air and spirits were high. TASTY agents were in the lab and they were facing off. 

    1963 was also the year Agent Marian Mayhand graduated at the top of her class. A specialist, she became a legend, nicknamed the Infantry for her outstanding missions. Her field career was glowing and looking to become even brighter, until the tragedy of 1977.

    Mayhand had been sent to take out a head of the TASTY animal experimentation called Arnold Z. Everything looked to be as expected until she arrived at the subject containment chambers, where the target was currently dissecting a live cow. Other cattle were being forced to watch this. Unknowingly, they had ruined the mission. Mayhand was behind her target, ready for a head shot, but the cattle saw her and mooed. He turned around and hit a red button, releasing a gas that made the bovines extremely angry and unlocking the cage doors. They all charged at her, but in accordance with EGGS beliefs she did not harm a single cow, instead interrogating Arnold Z. for the antidote, all the while dodging infuriated cows. She did not entirely succeed, and they were both very badly injured. Arnold Z. died before he gave the answer up, but the cattle calmed down after a few hours as the drug wore off. Mayhand sustained multiple injuries, putting her out of fieldwork forever. 

    Never one to give up, Mayhand went back to the Academy again as soon as she recovered to work in Communications, once more graduating at the top of her class. She quickly rose up the ranks, eventually directing the most complex operations, and working with some of the most influential people in EGGS' history, including Director Nikola MacCartson. In 1984, she was made Vice Director. 

    The year was 1971 and Oregano Caning had decided to retire. The problem was, he hadn't selected his replacement yet and there were no obvious candidates. So, he thought up the Paperwork Competition of 1971. More paperwork than Administration could handle was coming in and they were at their wit's end. Agents were still filing mission reports from months ago, and they needed to deal with it fast. Candidates were to fill out as much paperwork as possible in a period of 24 hours, and the person to do it with the most quality would get the job. Ten candidates were shortlisted, and with much coffee and tea, Agent Shan Shou-Wen won. A few years later, in 1980, Jäger Dove was fatally injured on a mission and died in a coma three days later. Quickly, Sanjay Adrian stepped in to take his place. 

    When MacCartson retired in June 1990, the position of Director was taken over by Marian Mayhand, her Vice Director Blake Jesper. Jack Tripousa retired the same year, as did Shan Shou-Wen. They were replaced by Wilhelmina Watson and Gregory Hooter. Mayhand formed the Dorg Alliance and set up the departments of BELL, HEN, and CHICK.  After analyzing the lastest fast food consumer statistics, she concluded that fast food chains were targeting children, as they were the new generation that grew up with fast food as something that had always been there and were too innocent to know the dangers of fast food. So she started to recruit children, in order to more efficiently battle TASTY. Sadly, her brilliant life was cut short as she was assassinated by a TASTY agent while delivering a speech. Her time as Director lasted just under a year.

    Blake Jesper took over as archdirector with Robert "Bobby" Michaels as his vice. Neither a field agent or a reporter, Jesper was an amazing strategist and solved problems with an ease that unnerved many people. At the time of MacCartson's death in 1997, her legacy was an international organization dedicated to fighting for animal rights with various funding, mainly from medical institutions and a few governments. Her funeral was quiet, fitting for the head of a spy agency. Multiple heads of ethics committees and such attended. In the same year, Fritz Simon retired and selected Link Gordon to fill his shoes. Under Jesper's guidance, EGGS became a well-oiled machine and shut down two TASTY parties: Marno McDermitt in 2003 and White Tower in 2004. Many agents were in awe of him and his stern appearance. Forty years old when he assumed the role of archdirector, he retired at the age of sixty in 2011.

    Michaels became the new archdirector, who is also the current one. For his first two years in office, it was a difficult period of EGGS' history, as fast food became more popular than ever; so his happy, optimist nature was very much needed. He was an inspiring orator and more recruits than ever joined, competing with the booming catering industry. The Vice Director from 2011-2013 was Dart Foster, who went MIA on a very classified operation. He was replaced with Lily Warden, a field agent, who, like MacCartson graduated at the top of her class. 

    At the age of seven, while on a road trip, a drunk truck driver crashed into her family car, killing her father and brother. Warden was paralyzed from the waist down. When she and her mother recovered, her mother became very depressed and took to drinking, and one day committed suicide by jumping in front of a truck, claiming "I wanted to be reunited with my family." Warden was twelve years old. She was passed around the foster care system, but nobody wanted a "defective" child. Everybody assumed she couldn't do anything in the way of sports, and she was determined to prove them wrong. Finally she did, becoming the captain of the basketball team and named the most valuable player for her school baseball team. During university, for her thesis she did a paper on the effects of fast-food, discovering many of the same facts MacCartson did half a decade ago, leading her to join EGGS.
